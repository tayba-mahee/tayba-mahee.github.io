## My Journey in CS Education

Teaching computer science is more than just conveying information—it's about fostering critical thinking, problem-solving skills, and a genuine passion for technology. Over my career teaching at multiple institutions, I've learned that effective CS education requires a careful balance of theory and hands-on practice.

## Teaching Philosophy

### Student-Centered Approach

I believe in placing students at the center of the learning process. This means:

- **Active Learning**: In-class practice problems and collaborative exercises
- **Formative Assessments**: Regular quizzes and assignments that provide immediate feedback
- **Flipped Classroom Elements**: Students engage with concepts before class, allowing more time for application

### Adapting to Diverse Learners

Computer science attracts students with varied backgrounds. Some key strategies I employ:

1. **Multiple Representations**: Presenting concepts through code, diagrams, and real-world analogies
2. **Scaffolded Learning**: Breaking complex topics into manageable chunks
3. **Office Hours & Support**: Providing individualized attention when needed

## Courses I Teach

### Discrete Structures

This foundational course covers logic, sets, functions, relations, and graph theory—essential mathematical foundations for computer science.

**Key Focus Areas:**
- Logic and proof techniques
- Combinatorics and counting
- Graph theory applications
- Recursive thinking

### Introduction to Computer Systems

A deep dive into how computers work at the hardware-software interface.

**Topics Covered:**
- Data representation
- Assembly language programming
- Memory hierarchy
- System-level programming in C

## Challenges and Rewards

### Common Challenges

- **Abstraction Difficulty**: Many students struggle with abstract mathematical concepts
- **Programming Anxiety**: Some students lack confidence in their coding abilities
- **Diverse Preparation**: Students come with varying levels of prior knowledge

### The Rewarding Moments

Despite challenges, teaching is incredibly fulfilling:

- Watching students have "aha!" moments
- Seeing learners gain confidence in problem-solving
- Receiving feedback about how course concepts helped in other classes or internships

## Looking Forward

Computer science education continues to evolve. I'm particularly interested in:

- **AI-Assisted Learning**: Using AI tools thoughtfully in education
- **Inclusive Pedagogy**: Ensuring all students feel welcome and capable
- **Industry Alignment**: Keeping curriculum relevant to real-world needs
