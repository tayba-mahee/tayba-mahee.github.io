## Overview

Women pursuing careers in STEM often rely on multiple disconnected platforms to search for jobs, connect with mentors, discover educational content, participate in communities, and attend professional events. This fragmented experience creates unnecessary friction throughout their professional journey.

**SheLeads** is an end-to-end mobile application concept designed to centralize these resources into a single platform. Rather than focusing solely on job discovery, the application supports continuous career development through mentorship, networking, learning, inspiration, and community engagement.

This project was developed as part of a UX design competition and marked my first experience designing a complete mobile product using **Figma**. It introduced me to the complete UX design process—from user research and ideation to prototyping and visual design.

# The Challenge

Women in STEM frequently switch between different platforms to:

- Search for jobs
- Find mentors
- Discover learning resources

> **How might we create a single platform that empowers women in STEM throughout every stage of their career journey?**

# Research

## Research Objectives

The primary objective was to understand how women currently navigate professional growth within STEM industries and identify opportunities to improve that experience through a unified digital product.

The research focused on:

- Understanding career development challenges
- Exploring mentorship behaviors
- Investigating networking habits
- Identifying learning opportunities

---

## User Research

Before designing the interface, I conducted exploratory user research to better understand the challenges women face when searching for jobs, mentorship opportunities, and career development resources within STEM fields.

A survey consisting of **12 questions** was distributed to participants between the ages of **23 and 35**, collecting **15 responses**. The survey explored current job-search behaviors, professional learning habits, mentorship needs, and perceptions of existing career development platforms.

![User Research](/assets/img/sheleads/user-research.png)

### Research Goals

- Understand how users currently search for STEM career opportunities.
- Identify challenges encountered during the job search process.
- Explore how users prepare for interviews and professional growth.
- Understand the demand for mentorship and career guidance.
- Evaluate existing platforms supporting women in technology.

### Sample Survey Questions

- How long have you been searching for a job?
- How do you prepare for your next interview?
- Have you used a career development application before?
- How available are women-focused career resources?
- Where do you find information about workplace culture and gender equality?
- Would you use a dedicated career development platform?

### Key Insights

The survey revealed several recurring themes:

- Users wanted a centralized platform dedicated to careers in STEM.
- Participants expressed a strong interest in mentorship and peer support.
- Existing platforms lacked curated learning resources tailored to career growth.
- Users wanted greater transparency regarding workplace culture and inclusivity.
- Career development resources were scattered across multiple websites, making them difficult to discover and navigate.

The findings established the foundation for the remainder of the design process and guided feature prioritization throughout the project.


## Competitive Analysis

To understand the existing landscape, I reviewed several websites and applications focused on women in technology, professional networking, and career development. The goal was to identify common design patterns, understand existing offerings, and uncover opportunities for improvement.

Rather than replicating existing solutions, I wanted to understand what users were still missing.

### Key Findings

Across the products analyzed, several usability and content gaps consistently emerged:

- Interfaces often felt visually cluttered, making important information difficult to locate.
- No single platform combined job opportunities, mentorship, learning resources, networking, and career guidance into one cohesive experience.
- Many blogs discussed general career topics but lacked content specifically focused on women working in technology.
- Existing women-in-tech resources were not consistently updated with current opportunities and industry insights.
- Most platforms focused primarily on job listings while offering few supporting educational or professional development resources.
- Dense layouts and overwhelming information reduced overall usability.
- Leadership stories and successful women in technology were often separated into standalone programs instead of being integrated into everyday career exploration.

### Design Opportunities

These observations directly influenced the direction of SheLeads.

The application was designed to:

- Consolidate career development resources into one platform.
- Combine jobs, mentorship, networking, learning, and inspiration.
- Present information through a clean, approachable interface.
- Support users throughout their professional journey rather than only during job searching.
- Surface inspiring stories and educational content alongside career opportunities.

# Visual Design

To create a welcoming and approachable experience, I developed a visual design system focused on clarity, consistency, and accessibility.

The interface uses soft pastel colors and generous whitespace to reduce visual clutter while maintaining a calm, professional aesthetic suitable for career development.

### Design Principles

- Maintain visual consistency across all screens.
- Use accessible color combinations with clear hierarchy.
- Create reusable UI components for scalability.
- Balance professional aesthetics with a friendly visual identity.

### Color Palette

The color system combines lavender, blue, mint, blush, and peach tones to create a modern and inclusive visual language while supporting clear information hierarchy.

### Typography

A consistent typography system was established to improve readability and maintain a cohesive visual identity throughout the application.

The resulting design system provided a reusable foundation for all interface components and supported rapid iteration during the prototyping phase.

![Visual Design System](/assets/img/sheleads/style-guide.png)

## Prototype

With the core user flows and interface structure established, I developed an interactive high-fidelity prototype in **Figma** to validate the overall user experience. The prototype focused on creating a seamless and intuitive journey that supports women in STEM throughout different stages of their career development.

Rather than designing isolated screens, I aimed to build a connected product experience that allows users to naturally transition between discovering opportunities, learning new skills, finding mentors, and engaging with professional communities.

### Core User Flows

The prototype includes several end-to-end user journeys, including:

- User onboarding and account creation
- Secure login and authentication
- Personalized home dashboard
- Job discovery and application browsing
- Expert and mentor discovery
- Appointment booking
- Learning resources and curated topics
- Articles, podcasts, and video recommendations
- Conference and event registration
- Women-in-tech communities and networking spaces

### Design Considerations

During prototyping, I focused on several key UX principles:

- **Clear navigation** through a simplified information hierarchy.
- **Consistent interaction patterns** across all primary features.
- **Progressive disclosure** to reduce cognitive load while keeping advanced content accessible.
- **Responsive layouts** optimized for mobile devices.
- **Reusable UI components** to maintain visual consistency and support future scalability.

The prototype also helped validate screen transitions, content organization, and task completion before finalizing the visual design system.

Overall, prototyping transformed the research findings into a cohesive mobile experience that balanced usability, accessibility, and engagement while supporting users throughout their professional growth journey.

This project fundamentally changed the way I approach product design.
