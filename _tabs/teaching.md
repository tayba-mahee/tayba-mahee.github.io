## Teaching Philosophy

<div class="philosophy-section">
  <p style="text-align: center; font-size: 1.1rem; max-width: 900px; margin: 0 auto 2rem; line-height: 1.7;">
    My teaching philosophy is grounded in <strong>care, clarity, practice, and adaptability</strong>—with a strong focus on helping students truly understand the material while feeling supported and confident.
  </p>

  <div class="philosophy-grid">
    <div class="philosophy-card">
      <span class="icon">👥</span>
      <h3>Student-Centered Learning</h3>
      <p>Creating supportive environments where students feel comfortable asking questions and making mistakes without fear of judgment.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="philosophy-card">
      <span class="icon">💡</span>
      <h3>Conceptual Understanding</h3>
      <p>Moving beyond memorization to deep comprehension through clear explanations, real-world examples, and multiple perspectives.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="philosophy-card">
      <span class="icon">🎯</span>
      <h3>Active Practice</h3>
      <p>Learning through consistent practice with regular homework, formative assessments, and hands-on programming assignments.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="philosophy-card">
      <span class="icon">🔄</span>
      <h3>Adaptive Teaching</h3>
      <p>Adjusting methods based on student feedback and diverse learning styles, using flipped classroom and flexible approaches.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

## Professional Development

I continuously work to improve my teaching through:
- Participating in pedagogy workshops and conferences at Baylor's Academy for Teaching and Learning
- Incorporating evidence-based teaching practices from education research
- Seeking and responding to student feedback through regular course evaluations and mid-semester check-ins
- Staying current with CS education research and best practices
- Attending workshops on classroom equity, transparent teaching methods, and serving diverse students

For insights into my teaching philosophy and methods, read my blog post on [Teaching Computer Science](/posts/teaching-computer-science/).
