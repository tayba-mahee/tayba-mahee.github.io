## Gesture Recognition Using Sign Language Data

**Domain:** Human-Computer Interaction & Computer Vision  
**Institution:** Baylor University  
**Duration:** 2022-2023

### Overview

This research focused on gesture recognition systems utilizing sign language datasets to develop accessible human-computer interaction technologies. The project explored computer vision techniques for real-time gesture classification and contributed to proposal development for extended research in accessible computing.

### Research Objectives

- Investigate state-of-the-art gesture recognition approaches
- Explore sign language recognition for accessibility applications
- Develop preprocessing pipelines for gesture datasets
- Contribute to research proposal development for NSF funding

### Research Activities

**Literature Review:**
- Surveyed existing gesture recognition methodologies
- Analyzed deep learning approaches for sign language recognition
- Studied temporal modeling techniques for sequential gesture data
- Reviewed accessibility technologies and their impact

**Dataset Preparation:**
- Explored multiple sign language datasets:
  - American Sign Language (ASL) datasets
  - Isolated and continuous gesture sequences
  - Multi-modal data (video, depth, skeletal tracking)
- Implemented preprocessing pipelines for data cleaning
- Created data augmentation strategies for robust training
- Developed annotation tools for gesture labeling

**Exploratory Analysis:**
- Conducted statistical analysis of gesture datasets
- Visualized gesture patterns and temporal dynamics
- Investigated feature extraction techniques
- Analyzed computational requirements for real-time recognition

**Proposal Development:**
- Contributed to research methodology design
- Helped formulate research questions and hypotheses
- Assisted in experimental design planning
- Participated in grant proposal writing activities

### Technical Approach

**Computer Vision Techniques:**
- Hand detection and tracking algorithms
- Pose estimation for gesture analysis
- Optical flow for motion detection
- Spatial-temporal feature extraction

**Potential Model Architectures:**
- 3D Convolutional Neural Networks (C3D)
- Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks
- Transformer-based sequence models
- Attention mechanisms for temporal modeling

### Research Insights

- Identified key challenges in real-time gesture recognition
- Recognized importance of temporal context in sign language
- Understood trade-offs between accuracy and computational efficiency
- Gained insights into accessibility technology design

### Technical Skills Developed

- Computer vision and video analysis
- Dataset preparation and preprocessing
- Literature review and research synthesis
- Research proposal development
- Interdisciplinary collaboration
- Understanding of accessibility technologies

### Future Directions

This research laid groundwork for potential extensions:
- Real-time sign language translation systems
- Multi-modal gesture recognition
- Edge device deployment for accessibility applications
- Cross-cultural sign language recognition
