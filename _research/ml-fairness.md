## Machine Learning Robustness, Fairness, and Performance Study

**Domain:** AI Ethics & Machine Learning Theory  
**Institution:** Baylor University  
**Duration:** 2022-2023  
**Funding:** NSF Proposal Research

### Overview

This research contributed to an NSF proposal studying the complex relationships between machine learning model robustness, fairness, and performance. The project investigated fundamental questions about trade-offs in AI systems and their implications for deploying fair and reliable machine learning in real-world applications.

### Research Objectives

- Investigate trade-offs between model robustness, fairness, and accuracy
- Analyze model performance across different demographic groups
- Develop methodologies for testing fairness-robustness relationships
- Contribute to responsible AI and ethical machine learning research

### Research Activities

**Literature Review:**
- Surveyed fairness definitions in machine learning:
  - Demographic parity
  - Equal opportunity
  - Equalized odds
  - Individual fairness
- Reviewed robustness research:
  - Adversarial robustness
  - Distribution shift resilience
  - Out-of-distribution generalization
- Analyzed existing fairness-performance trade-off studies

**Experimental Design:**
- Designed experiments to test fairness-robustness-accuracy trade-offs
- Developed evaluation protocols for multi-objective assessment
- Created benchmark datasets with demographic information
- Planned systematic testing across multiple model architectures

**Fairness Analysis:**
- Analyzed model performance across demographic groups
- Measured disparate impact and differential performance
- Investigated sources of bias in training data
- Explored fairness intervention techniques:
  - Pre-processing (data debiasing)
  - In-processing (fairness-aware training)
  - Post-processing (output calibration)

**Performance Evaluation:**
- Implemented fairness metrics:
  - Statistical parity difference
  - Equal opportunity difference
  - Disparate impact ratio
- Measured robustness to adversarial examples
- Evaluated generalization across domains
- Assessed calibration and uncertainty quantification

### Key Research Questions

1. **Trade-off Analysis:**
   - What is the relationship between fairness and robustness?
   - Can models be simultaneously fair and robust?
   - How does improving one objective affect the others?

2. **Intervention Strategies:**
   - Which fairness interventions preserve robustness?
   - How do regularization techniques affect fairness?
   - Can adversarial training improve fairness?

3. **Real-World Implications:**
   - How do these trade-offs manifest in deployment?
   - What are the societal implications of these relationships?
   - How should practitioners balance competing objectives?
